Research Program

Phenotypic innovation, read through the genome

We ask how new ecological ways of life — a new diet, a new signal, a new social role — get written into a genome. Tropical insects give us repeated, independent natural experiments in innovation, and we build the comparative genomic tools needed to read them.

System 01 · Heliconiini

Heliconius pollen-feeding

Heliconius butterflies are the only lepidopterans known to actively collect and digest pollen, extracting amino acids that extend adult lifespan and reproductive output far beyond related nectar-feeding butterflies.

We study the genomic architecture behind this innovation: which gene families expanded or were co-opted, how regulatory regions shifted, and how convergent molecular changes track the repeated evolution of related traits across Heliconiini.

Clade Heliconiini Species scale 60+ genomes Collaborators S. Montgomery, W. O. McMillan
System 02 · Riodinidae

Riodinid–ant associations

Many riodinid (metalmark) butterfly caterpillars survive by convincing ants they belong — through chemical mimicry, tactile signals, and substrate-borne vibrational communication that mirror the ants' own social language. It's a system where a butterfly larva effectively hacks another insect society's recognition code.

We're interested in the genomic basis of these mimicry systems: how cuticular hydrocarbon profiles and sound/vibration-producing structures evolve to track ant host specificity, and what this tells us about the tempo and mechanism of interspecies signal convergence.

Focus chemical & acoustic mimicry Collaborators A. Freitas, L. Kaminski
System 03 · Meliponini

Stingless bee cleptobiosis

Some stingless bee lineages have abandoned foraging altogether in favour of raiding the nests of other bee species for stored resources — a form of social parasitism called cleptobiosis. It's an unusual route to a division-of-labour society, built on theft rather than collection.

We study how caste determination, colony chemistry, and behavioural repertoires diverge between cleptobiotic lineages and their foraging relatives, using comparative genomics and single-cell transcriptomics to trace the molecular signature of this shift in social strategy.

Focus social parasitism · caste evolution
Infrastructure

Methods built for genomes without a manual

Genome assembly

Long-read comparative genomics

Reference-quality assemblies and whole-genome alignments across non-model insect clades with no prior genomic resources.

Orthology & phylogenomics

Ortholog rescue & gene-tree inference

BUSCO-based pipelines with paralogy filtering, C-family mixture models in IQ-TREE, and topology testing across competing phylogenetic hypotheses.

Single-cell & EcoEvoDevo

Linking genome to phenotype

Single-cell transcriptomics and developmental biology connect genomic signatures of selection to the traits they actually build.
