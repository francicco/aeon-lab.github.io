Principal Investigator

Francesco Cicconardi

InTheGra Fellow, USP São Paulo · School of Biological Sciences, University of Bristol

Francesco leads the Adaptive Evolution of Novelty Lab, working at the intersection of comparative genomics, EcoEvoDevo, and single-cell transcriptomics to understand how tropical insects evolve new ways of living. His work spans genome assembly and phylogenomics across non-model insect clades, with a particular focus on Heliconius butterflies, riodinid–ant associations, and stingless bee social evolution.

He currently holds an InTheGra Fellowship at USP São Paulo, funded by FAPESP (co-PI Prof. Maria Cristina Arias), alongside an ongoing affiliation with the University of Bristol's School of Biological Sciences, where he collaborates closely with Prof. Stephen H. Montgomery.
